# Analysis

- Source: `https://blogs.newardassociates.com/blog/2023/you-want-modules-not-microservices.html`
- Source language: English
- Target language: `zh-CN`
- Mode: `refined`
- Audience: `general`
- Style: `storytelling`

## Content type

An architecture opinion essay. It critiques the hype around microservices, distinguishes modular decomposition from distributed deployment, and reframes the real benefit as team autonomy and organizational clarity.

## Tone

- Sharp
- Skeptical
- Historically grounded
- Slightly sarcastic

## Translation priorities

1. Preserve the argumentative rhythm and sarcasm.
2. Keep technical references precise.
3. Avoid over-softening the author's criticism.
4. Keep section headers punchy because they are part of the article's rhetorical structure.

## Reader comprehension challenges

- The article references older technologies such as EJB, Oracle Tuxedo, CORBA, SOAP, WSDL, and CASE tools.
- The rhetorical comparison between modules and microservices depends on architectural history, so some readers may need brief translator notes.
- The author's sarcasm should remain readable in Chinese without turning too colloquial.

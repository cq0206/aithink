## Reader comprehension challenges

- The article mixes product design, frontend engineering, and prompt-writing advice.
- Terms like `hero`, `full-bleed`, `design tokens`, and `utility copy` may be unfamiliar to general readers.
- The embedded "frontend skill" is written like prompt configuration, so formatting must stay clean and readable in Chinese.

## Figurative or strategy-sensitive language

- "Delightful" is not just "令人愉悦"; here it implies polished, intentional, premium-feeling UI.
- "Start with composition, not components" should be translated as a design strategy, not a literal instruction about code structure.
- "Treat the first viewport as a poster, not a document" is metaphorical; preserve the visual-design implication.
